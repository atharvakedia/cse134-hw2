Extra Credit — MathML

Mass-energy equivalence, written with native <math> markup:

E=mc2

MathML has technically been part of the web platform since the late 1990s, and Firefox and Safari have supported it natively for most of that time — but Chrome (and every other Chromium-based browser, including Edge) effectively couldn’t render it for most of its history. Chrome briefly shipped experimental MathML support in version 24 (2013) and then pulled it again due to stability issues. It wasn’t until Chrome 109, in January 2023, that native MathML (via the newer MathML Core spec) landed for real. So for about a decade, “use MathML” meant either accepting broken rendering for most visitors or reaching for a JavaScript polyfill like MathJax — which is exactly the kind of gap caniuse.com is useful for catching before you rely on a feature.
